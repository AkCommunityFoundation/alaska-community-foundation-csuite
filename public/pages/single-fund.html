Dylan Slyker Memorial Fund

In memory of Dylan Slyker.


Dylan Fischer Slyker was born in Anchorage, Alaska and then thrived in Denver, Colorado before leaving this earth March 1st, 2025. He was a junior at CU Denver and was just 21 years old. Dylan was an avid skier, remarkable basketball player, passionate fly fisherman, had a veracious appetite for food and life, loved and appreciated music of all genres, was an incredible writer and artist, had an amazing friend group here at home and at school, was in love with his beautiful girlfriend, and was the very best big brother and kiddo a family has ever known.

Dylan was a fighter from day one, with a difficult birth, but then grew into a strong young man. Unfortunately, Dylan also struggled with his mental health and depression for much of his young life. He worked incredibly hard, with the support of our entire family, to face it head-on. He worked with therapists and doctors, took medication and supplements, exercised daily, was training for a full marathon, journaled and created art to help relieve the pressure. But the pain was always real for him, and we find comfort knowing our brilliant boy has finally found peace.

We ask people to be like Dylan going forward. Be strong like Dylan. Be brilliant like Dylan. Be genuine like Dylan. Mental health is something that must be discussed and addressed.

Our family hopes this memorial fund will help others dealing with their own mental health and raise awareness for something that so many battle each and every day. Additionally, Dylan never wanted his mental health to define him. In that spirit, this fund will also celebrate the things Dylan loved most, and where he found solace, in things such as friendship, music, art and basketball. Because these things are Dylan.

We take great comfort in knowing that Dylan knows how much we love him, and how deeply his friends and extended family love him. And we know how much Dylan loves us. Fiercely, as he does all things.

Dylan, we will endure a lifetime of missing you for the privilege of loving you.
